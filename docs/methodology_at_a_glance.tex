\paragraph{Methodology at a Glance.}
We evaluate unseen-state discovery for IQP and classical generators on a score-tilted even-parity target family over \(\{0,1\}^{12}\), using fixed-size holdout sets that are removed from training and recovered under controlled sample budgets.
Our primary protocol is global-scope holdout with smart selection (mass-aware filtering plus Hamming-diverse subset construction), and we treat global-random holdout as a sensitivity protocol.
Discovery is quantified by holdout recovery curves \(R(Q)\), threshold budget \(Q_{80}\), and holdout mass \(q(H)\), while distribution fit metrics are reported as secondary diagnostics.
To connect mechanism to performance, we use parity-feature spectral analysis (\(q_{\mathrm{lin}}\), visibility, simplex completion), verify expected visibility scaling identities under random subset sampling, and compare predicted vs measured recovery behavior under matched seeds, data, and training budget classes.
